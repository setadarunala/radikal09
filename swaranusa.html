SWARA NUSA

Swastiastu sareng sami,
Atur titian raine mangkin Magenjekan gaya guyu Ngilangin sakit hati,
mengalunang jiwa seni

(I Kadek Dwi Santika-Swara Nusa)

Swara Nusa is an ancient Indonesian (Malay) language which means the sound of archipelago or the sound of the children of archipelago. They are derivated from the old name of Indonesia, Nuswantara/Nusantara. Those two words—Nuswa/Nusa and Antara—shows the area of Indonesia archipelago which extends from the city of Sabang (the western border of Indonesia) to Merauke (the eastern border of Indonesia). This name, Swara Nusa—whose group was made in the first 2016, also holds our objection which is to show the beauty of the musical multiplicity existing in this Nation (Nusantara).

Six musicians of Swara Nusa has different ethnical backgrounds, even though they were born within the same nationality (Nusantara/Indonesia). Pioneered by I Kadek Dwi Santika from Bali, he gathered five others members, which are Kadek Anggara Rismandika (Bali), Primadini Dwi Maulinda (Banyuwangi), Primadana Affandi (East Borneo), Muhammad Fabian Ar-rizqy (Lampung), and Frendy Satria Palindo (Padang).

This group is still in its creative process for making the awesome collaborative music works supervised by Radikal Nol Sembilan Art Organizer (Person in Charge: Brian Trinanda K. A.—Representative Manager of Radikal Nol Sembilan).
